Original Midrashic Works and Commentaries

Explore our collection of original Jewish texts written in both English and Hebrew. These works combine classical sources with modern insights to provide comprehensive study materials on midrash aggadah topics.

All texts are available for free reading and download.

Darosh Darash Moshe

Language: English

Focus: The life, character, and legacy of Moshe Rabbeinu through three ascents in the desert

A comprehensive modern midrashic work that explores Moshe Rabbeinu's spiritual journey and leadership. Building upon classical aggadic sources, this sefer develops new insights into the character and teachings of the greatest of all prophets.

Features:

  • Detailed analysis of Moshe's three ascents
  • Integration of classical and modern sources
  • Comprehensive footnotes and bibliography
  • Available in PDF format

Midrash HaAliyah (מדרש העלייה)

Language: Hebrew

Focus: Hebrew companion work to Darosh Darash Moshe

A midrashic work written in classical Hebrew style, serving as a companion to Darosh Darash Moshe. This sefer summarizes and expands upon the themes in the style of traditional aggadic literature, combining teachings of חז״ל with original insights and חידושים.

Features:

  • Written in traditional Hebrew style
  • Comprehensive source citations
  • Original insights (חידושים)
  • Complete bibliography in Hebrew

Commentary on Ein Yaakov (פירוש על העין יעקב)

Language: Hebrew and Aramaic

Focus: Original commentary on aggadic sections of the Talmud

חידושים on the aggadic sections of Seder Nezikin, Seder Kodashim, and Seder Toharot. Written in the traditional style of Talmudic commentaries, combining Hebrew and Aramaic. Based on insights developed through hundreds of shiurim on Ein Yaakov.

Features:

  • Traditional commentary style
  • Focus on three specific sedarim
  • Based on extensive shiur preparation
  • Hebrew and Aramaic text

About These Works

These sefarim represent years of study and teaching in the field of midrash aggadah. They are offered freely to the Jewish learning community to promote the study and appreciation of aggadic literature.

Each work can be read online or downloaded for offline study. We encourage sharing these materials to increase הרבצת התורה (spreading of Torah).
